data-file-height="2624" /></a></div>\n</div>\n<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Paxillus_involutus" title="Paxillus involutus">Paxillus involutus</a></b></i>, the common roll-rim, is a <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungus</a> widely distributed across the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>; it has also been unintentionally <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced</a> to Australia, New Zealand, and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. The brownish <a href="/wiki/Basidiocarp" title="Basidiocarp">fruit body</a> grows up to 6&#160;cm (2.4&#160;in) high. It has a funnel-shaped <a href="/wiki/Pileus_(mycology)" title="Pileus (mycology)">cap</a> up to 12&#160;cm (5&#160;in) wide with a distinctive in-rolled rim and <a href="/wiki/Decurrent" title="Decurrent">decurrent</a> gills close to the <a href="/wiki/Stipe_(mycology)" title="Stipe (mycology)">stalk</a>. Genetic testing suggests that the fungus may be a <a href="/wiki/Species_complex" title="Species complex">species complex</a> rather than a single species. A common mushroom of <a href="/wiki/Temperate_deciduous_forest" title="Temperate deciduous forest">deciduous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Temperate_coniferous_forest" title="Temperate coniferous forest">coniferous</a> woods and grassy areas in late summer and autumn, <i>P. involutus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Mycorrhiza#Ectomycorrhiza" title="Mycorrhiza">symbiotic with the roots</a> of many tree species, reducing the trees\' intake of <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_(chemistry)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavy metal (chemistry)">heavy metals</a> and increasing their resistance to pathogens. Previously considered <a href="/wiki/Edible_mushroom" title="Edible mushroom">edible</a> and eaten widely in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a>, the mushroom has been found to be dangerously <a href="/wiki/Poisonous_mushroom" class="mw-redirect" title="Poisonous mushroom">poisonous</a>; in 1944, it killed the German <a href="/wiki/Mycologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Mycologist">mycologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Julius_Sch%C3%A4ffer" title="Julius Sch\xc3\xa4ffer">Julius Sch\xc3\xa4ffer</a>. It can trigger the <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a> to attack red blood cells with potentially fatal complications, including acute <a href="/wiki/Renal_failure#Acute_renal_failure" class="mw-redirect" title="Renal failure">renal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_failure" title="Respiratory failure">respiratory failure</a>.
